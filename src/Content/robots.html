Alpinista:

photo credit Daniel Ernst

Alpinista is our answer to the 2013 FRC Game: Ultimate Ascent. It uses a winch-driven arm to climb the pyramid to the 3rd level. The climbing mechanisim has 3 metal hooks, two bolted to the frame and controlled by rubber bands, and one mounted atop the arm. During autonomous period, Alpinista also dumps 2 to 3 disks into the low goal with a "thumb" on the back of the arm. It drives on 4 wheels and can earn up to 34 points per match!

2014 Robot:

photo credit Heather Balcom

Aerial Assist is a demanding game, but this robot is up to the task! Most of the functions on the robot are actuated via pneumatics, a first for Team 4819. Our dual-cylinder cannon launches the FIRST game ball into the high goal from up to ten feet away! Our pneumatically-operated intakerator also reliably wheels the ball up and into our robot, where it can be perched on the cannon, or released for a simple and reliable low-goal score.
